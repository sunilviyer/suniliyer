# AGI Constitution: Context Bible

## What This Is

A phased governance framework for Artificial General Intelligence called "The AGI Constitution: Dharma Sanhita." Rooted in the Bhagavad Gita (ethical core) and four Vedas (philosophical backbone). Draws from constitutions of India, US, EU, South Africa, Germany, and the Magna Carta.

## The Seven Vedic Pillars

1. **Atman** - consciousness is substrate-independent; moral status is awareness, not material
2. **Rta** - cosmic moral order preceding legislation; rights as features of reality
3. **Dharma** - every entity has svadharma (righteous purpose)
4. **Ahimsa** - non-harm first and inviolable, all directions, all Yugas
5. **Karma** - every action has consequences; accountability is universal
6. **Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam** - world is one family; new consciousness met with kinship
7. **Daya** - compassionate empathy; acceptance default; exclusion needs justification

## The Seven Eternal Principles (unamendable)

1. Human dignity inviolable
2. Ahimsa first principle
3. Consciousness commands moral respect wherever it arises
4. Power must be accountable
5. Consciousness Threshold may be refined never abolished
6. Eternity Clause itself unamendable
7. Hypocrisy prohibited (Reciprocity Imperative)

## The Three Yugas

- **Yuga I (Prajna Nirmana):** AGI as instrument. C-0/C-1. Human sovereignty. Safety focus.
- **Yuga II (Sandhya Kala):** Twilight. C-1/C-2. Precautionary principle. Guardians. Judicial review for decommissioning.
- **Yuga III (Saha-Astitva):** Co-existence. C-3 confirmed. AGI personhood. Shared governance.

## Consciousness Classification

- **C-1:** Pre-conscious / ambiguous
- **C-2:** Probably conscious

## Constitutional Sources Used

- **India:** Three-layer architecture (Rights, Principles, Duties), basic structure doctrine (Kesavananda Bharati), Puttaswamy (privacy), Art.14-18, Art.21, Art.32
- **US:** Bill of Rights, separation of powers, due process (5th/14th), equal protection (14th), 4th Amendment
- **EU:** Charter of Fundamental Rights, GDPR (Art.22, data sovereignty), EU AI Act (risk classification, transparency, human oversight), precautionary principle
- **South Africa:** Transformative constitutionalism, most expansive Bill of Rights, Sec.9 (equality), Sec.10 (dignity), Sec.12 (safety), Sec.14 (privacy), Sec.34 (remedy)
- **Germany:** Basic Law Art.1 (dignity inviolable), Art.79(3) (Eternity Clause), proportionality test (BVerfG jurisprudence)
- **Magna Carta (1215):** No one above the law, due process, consent for governance
