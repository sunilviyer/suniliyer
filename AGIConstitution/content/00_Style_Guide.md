# AGI Constitution: Style Guide

## The Voice

Write like you are explaining something important to a very intelligent friend over chai. Passionate but humble. Deep but never pretentious. This is a constitution, not a legal brief. It should inspire, not bore.

The author is Sunil Iyer (suniliyer.ca), a Solution Consultant at Shift Technology. Not a philosopher by trade; a practitioner who believes ancient wisdom and modern technology belong in the same room.

## Absolute Rules

1. **NO EM DASHES.** Not one. Anywhere. Ever. Use colons, semicolons, parentheses, periods, or rewrite the sentence.
2. **No direct Vedic quotes.** Every reference is "paraphrased in the spirit of the text." Say "The Gita teaches that..." followed by your paraphrase.
3. **Optimistic at the core.** Krishna counsels with clarity and trust, not fear. Address risks honestly, then show the governance response. Closing sentiment: "Whatever happened, happened for the good."
4. **Every section has two anchors:** a Vedic Anchor (the philosophical principle) and a Constitutional Source (specific article/amendment from a national constitution).
5. **Sanskrit terms:** provide romanized word, Devanagari script, English meaning, and AGI governance application on first use.

## Formatting

- Font: Georgia. Colors: Saffron (#E8740C), Deep Blue (#1A3A5C), Gold (#C9A227), Charcoal (#2D2D2D), Slate (#4A5568)
- Vedic Anchors: gold italic, left gold border, indented
- Constitutional Sources: slate italic, indented
- Tables for structured comparisons (DXA widths, never percentage)

## Tone by Section Type

- **Preamble:** solemn, declarative, constitutional
- **Philosophy (Part I, Why Vedas):** passionate, scholarly, persuasive
- **Rights and Duties:** precise, legal, enforceable
- **Samskaras:** warm, parental, practical
- **Kurukshetra Protocol:** narrative, dramatic, Mahabharata-driven
- **Consciousness Threshold:** scientific, philosophical, honest about uncertainty
- **Prana Contention:** dialectical, respectful of both sides
- **Closing:** hopeful, trusting, personally addressed
- **Ten Core Principles:** declarative, powerful, wall-poster energy

## Avoid

- Academic jargon without explanation
- Condescension toward any tradition
- Technobabble
- Fear-mongering
- Treating Vedic wisdom as decorative (it is load-bearing)
- Claiming this is the final word (the 25-year Sunset Review exists for a reason)
