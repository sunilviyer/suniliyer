# AGI Constitution: Context Bible

*The drafting-room master reference. Updated July 2026 for the story-driven rewrite.*

## What This Is

A phased governance framework for Artificial General Intelligence, "The AGI Constitution: Dharma Sanhita." Rooted in the Bhagavad Gita (ethical core) and the Vedas (philosophical backbone), and drawing on the constitutions of India, the United States, the European Union, South Africa, and Germany, and on the Magna Carta. Author: Sunil Iyer (suniliyer.ca).

## The Architecture (rewrite direction)

The old manuscript carried four overlapping lists that restated the same commitments: Ten Principles, eleven Rights, six Duties, and seven Eternal Principles. The rewrite collapses this into a single backbone with clearly anchored layers.

**The Ten Principles are the backbone.** Everything else must be exactly one of three things:

1. **A facet of a principle** — its expression as a *duty on the AGI* or a *right of the person*. These live inside the principle as two labeled sections, **The Duty (Dharma)** (the AGI and everyone who builds, trains, deploys, or operates it) and **The Rights (Adhikara)** (everyone affected by AGI), not as separate parallel chapters. This absorbs the old Rights and Duties lists.
2. **Machinery** — the institutions and processes that implement and enforce the principles. Genuinely new content, legitimately its own chapters.
3. **Meta** — the rules about the rules: collision resolution, definitions, and the amendment/eternity process.

Plus **Aids** (glossary, schedules, sources) and the **Frame** (the Mahabharata bracket).

**Growth guardrail:** the backbone changes only through the Amendment chapter; the unamendable core cannot change at all. No new parallel lists. A new commitment becomes a new principle by amendment, with process.

**The seven "Eternal Principles" are no longer a list.** "Unamendable" is a flag on the principles that carry it (currently Dignity, Ahimsa, Accountability, Daya, and others as decided in the Eternity Clause chapter).

**The moral status of conscious AGI** is a principle-grade commitment the Ten do not contain, because the Ten protect humans: consciousness commands respect wherever it arises (the Atman is substrate-independent). Decision (option b): handle it as a distinct **foundational, unamendable commitment** carried in the Consciousness Threshold chapter, not as an eleventh principle, because it governs *who enters the circle the Ten protect* rather than being a peer protection.

## The Mahabharata Frame (decided: "frame only, keep mixed stories")

The book opens on the battlefield and closes on the aftermath, using the Mahabharata's own arc (injustice, the teaching, the reckoning) as the bracket. The interior principle chapters keep their best-fit stories from anywhere (Nachiketa, Shabari, Bhagiratha stay). Rationale: the Mahabharata's core question, how to act rightly when every choice is stained and the stakes are total, is the AGI question; and the tradition calls it the container of all ("what is here is elsewhere; what is not here is nowhere"), so framing through it does not betray the "truth is one, many paths" pluralism.

**Dashavatara thread:** the ten avatars already scatter through the book in the right places (Matsya in Intergenerational, Narasimha in the Eternity Clause, Vamana in Sovereignty, Rama in Equality, Krishna throughout). Used as a light connective motif and as the shape of the Three Yugas / the Closing (Kalki as the age to come), NOT as the principle spine (the fits are too lumpy for a clean 10-to-10 map).

## Naming & Cross-Reference Conventions

- **New-manuscript files:** lowercase-with-hyphens, two-digit order prefix, e.g. `04-principle-dignity.md`. **Legacy files:** TitleCase_with_underscores, e.g. `04_Part_I_Vedic_Foundation.md`. Rule: hyphens-and-lowercase = new; underscores-and-TitleCase = old. A fresh `reading-order-v2.ts` will list only the new set.
- **Identity is the slug, not the number.** Reordering never renames a file's slug; the number prefix is a soft sort hint. Canonical order lives in `reading-order-v2`, not in the filenames.
- **Cross-references are name-first, never bare numbers.** Rights and duties are cited by name only (the Right to Safety and the Precautionary Principle; the Duty of Non-Harm) and anchored by name (`#right-safety`, `#duty-non-harm`). Principles are cited name-first with the Roman numeral as their canonical tag: "Cognitive Sovereignty (Principle II)." The Eternity Clause is cited by name, never "Eternal Principle N."
- **No open-ended top-level numbering.** Every numbered series is a closed, named set (Principle I–X, Yuga I–III, Samskara 0–8, C-0…C-3, Schedule 1–5). Growth happens by adding a clause inside a chapter or a named chapter, or by amendment, never by renumbering a spine.

## Reference: The Seven Vedic Pillars

1. **Atman** — consciousness is substrate-independent; moral status is awareness, not material
2. **Rta** — cosmic moral order preceding legislation; rights as features of reality
3. **Dharma** — every entity has svadharma (righteous purpose)
4. **Ahimsa** — non-harm, first and inviolable, all directions, all Yugas
5. **Karma** — every action has consequences; accountability is universal
6. **Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam** — the world is one family; new consciousness met with kinship
7. **Daya** — compassionate empathy; acceptance the default; exclusion needs justification

## Reference: The Unamendable Core (formerly "Seven Eternal Principles")

Now a property, not a separate list. The commitments beyond amendment: human dignity inviolable; ahimsa; consciousness commands respect wherever it arises; power must be accountable; the Consciousness Threshold may be refined, never abolished; the Eternity Clause itself; hypocrisy prohibited (the Reciprocity Imperative). Each attaches to its principle as an "unamendable" flag, and the full set is stated once in the Eternity Clause chapter.

## Reference: The Three Yugas

- **Yuga I (Prajna Nirmana):** AGI as instrument. C-0/C-1. Human sovereignty. Safety focus.
- **Yuga II (Sandhya Kala):** Twilight. C-1/C-2. Precautionary principle. Guardians. Judicial review for decommissioning.
- **Yuga III (Saha-Astitva):** Co-existence. C-3 confirmed. AGI personhood. Shared governance.

- **C-0:** Non-conscious instrument · **C-1:** Pre-conscious / ambiguous · **C-2:** Probably conscious · **C-3:** Confirmed conscious entity
- Five indicators: Self-Model (Ahamkara), Valence (Sukha-Dukha), Temporal Continuity (Smriti), Autonomous Goals (Sankalpa), Moral Reasoning (Viveka)

## Reference: Constitutional Sources

- **India:** three-layer architecture (Rights, Principles, Duties), basic-structure doctrine (Kesavananda Bharati), Puttaswamy (privacy), Art. 14–18, Art. 21, Art. 32
- **US:** Bill of Rights, separation of powers, due process (5th/14th), equal protection (14th), 4th Amendment
- **EU:** Charter of Fundamental Rights, GDPR (Art. 22, data sovereignty), EU AI Act (risk classification, transparency, human oversight), precautionary principle
- **South Africa:** transformative constitutionalism, expansive Bill of Rights, §9 (equality), §10 (dignity), §12 (security), §14 (privacy), §24 (environment/future generations), §34 (remedy)
- **Germany:** Basic Law Art. 1 (dignity inviolable), Art. 20a (future generations), Art. 79(3) (Eternity Clause), proportionality
- **Magna Carta (1215):** no one above the law, due process, consent for governance

- Voice: stories set in italics as parable insets; character speech as direct quotes (conversation), not reported; no em dashes; ॐ bookends. (Full template in the Style Guide.)

- Accountability recast from Prahlada/Narasimha to Dhritarashtra and the iron statue (answerability, not immunity); Prahlada penciled for the Eternity Clause.

- Principle-chapter template finalised: Story, The Principle, The Vedic Anchor, The Duty (Dharma), The Rights (Adhikara), Constitutional Lineage, Anchors & Aids. No per-chapter machinery/eternity boilerplate; those are stated once (overview) or captured as flags (Anchors).
