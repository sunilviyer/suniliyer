## 1. Project Overview

### Vision
A visual journey through the Bhagavad Gita, presenting its wisdom through 10 pivotal moments. The experience transforms from darkness to light‚Äîmirroring Arjuna's journey from despair to illumination.

### Core Principles
- **Content is king** ‚Äî The verses, translations, and interpretations are the heart
- **Visual as atmosphere** ‚Äî Art and animation create mood, not distraction
- **Layered depth** ‚Äî Surface beauty for casual visitors, deep meaning for seekers
- **Single page experience** ‚Äî All 10 moments accessible without navigation fatigue

### What This Is NOT
- Not a complete translation of all 18 chapters
- Not an academic commentary
- Not a religious instruction manual
- Not a linear chapter-by-chapter walkthrough

### Target Audience
- People curious about the Gita but intimidated by its length
- Those seeking practical wisdom, not religious doctrine
- Design/art appreciators who will be drawn by the visuals
- Portfolio visitors who want to see thoughtful, meaningful work

## 2. Content Architecture

### Structure: "Moments, Not Chapters"

Instead of 18 chapters, we curate **10 pivotal moments**. Each moment is a self-contained micro-experience that can stand alone or connect to the whole.

### Why 10 Moments?

| Consideration | Reasoning |
|---------------|-----------|
| **Comprehensiveness** | Covers all major paths (Karma, Jnana, Bhakti, Dhyana) |
| **Production feasibility** | Each needs art, animation, layered content |
| **Visitor attention** | Digestible without overwhelming |
| **Narrative arc** | Complete journey from crisis to resolution |

### Content Layers (Per Moment)

Each moment unfolds in 4 layers:

| Layer | Content | Tone |
|-------|---------|------|
| **Layer 0** | Visual + Sanskrit (Devanagari) + Transliteration | Sacred, atmospheric |
| **Layer 1** | English translation + brief context | Clear, accessible |
| **Layer 2** | The inner/psychological meaning | Contemplative |
| **Layer 3** | Practical application ("For You, Today") | Conversational, direct |

### Content Sources

| Element | Source Approach |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Sanskrit (Devanagari)** | Standard texts |
| **Transliteration** | IAST standard |
| **English Translation** | Curate from Easwaran, Gita Press, or Bibek Debroy ‚Äî pick ONE for consistency |
| **Layer 2-3 Discussion** | Original writing in Sunil's voice |

### Color Application

| Element | Color(s) | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|

| Background (per moment) | Shifts with selection | Radial gradient from accent color |
| Sanskrit verses | Amber Flame | Glowing, sacred |
| Transliteration | Orange | Secondary sacred |
| English translation | White (#FFFFFF) | Clean, readable |
| Body text (layers) | Light gray (#CCCCCC) | Softer, contemplative |
| UI labels | Khand font, Orange/Amber | Crisp, modern |
| Hover states | Accent ‚Üí brighter | Warmth rising |

### Art Style Direction

**Primary Influence:** Traditional Indian miniature painting (Rajput, Pahari, Mughal)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Minimalist composition ‚Äî clean negative space
- Limited color palette within each image
- Muted earth tones with gold accents
- Simplified but recognizable forms
- Flat perspective (traditional, not Western realism)
- Contemplative mood, not action-oriented

**What to Avoid:**
- Photorealistic rendering
- Western fantasy art styles
- Overly busy compositions
- Bright, saturated modern colors
- Generic "AI art" aesthetic

## 6. UX & Interaction Design

### Core Concept: Two Distinct Modes

The interface has two clean modes that never overlap. Navigate OR read ‚Äî not both at once.

| Mode | Purpose | What's Visible |
|------|---------|----------------|
| **Navigation** | Choose a moment | Chakra, master image, footer |
| **Reading** | Experience a moment | Content, scene image, position dots, close button, footer |

This separation ensures:
- The chakra isn't competing with content
- Images get full visual attention
- Clear mental model for the user
- Same logic scales across all devices

## 7. Visual Production

### Finalized Art Direction

**Style:** Abstract watercolor, wet-on-wet technique, contemporary Indian art influence

**Key Characteristics:**
- Figures dissolving at edges, not sharply defined
- Watercolor bleeding and pooling into handmade paper texture
- Emotional weight conveyed through form, not detail
- Vast negative space (cream/off-white paper)
- Minimalist ‚Äî only essential elements

**Color Palette:**
- Earth tones: burnt sienna, ochre, amber, brown
- Blue: indigo, cerulean (for Krishna)
- Gold/amber for divine radiance
- Cream paper as background

**What Works:**
- Abstract suggestion of figures
- Emotional posture over anatomical accuracy  
- Watercolor bleeds conveying feeling (grief drips down, radiance spreads out)
- Small human figures for scale against the vast/divine

**What to Avoid:**
- Multiple arms/eyes (clich√©)
- Horror or terror imagery
- Overly literal depictions
- Busy compositions
- Sharp, defined edges
