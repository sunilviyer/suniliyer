## Amazon AI Case Studies Identified

### 1. The Hiring Algorithm Controversy (Primary Case Study)

**Context**: Amazon's most frequently referenced AI incident
**Appears in**: 15+ articles

**The Story**:
Amazon built an AI recruiting tool to screen resumes, trained on 10 years of historical hiring data. The system learned from past hiring patterns that favored men in tech roles and began penalizing resumes containing female-associated terms:

- Downgraded resumes containing "women's" (as in "women's chess club captain")
- Penalized candidates from women's colleges
- Systematically discriminated against female applicants

**Discovery & Response**:
- Amazon discovered the bias during internal testing
- The company scrapped the tool before widespread deployment
- Became a landmark case study in AI bias and discrimination

**Governance Lessons**:
- Historical data can embed societal biases
- AI systems can amplify discrimination at scale
- Regular bias testing is essential
- Diverse training data and testing teams are crucial

**Legal Implications**:
- Demonstrates potential Title VII violations
- Shows need for algorithmic auditing
- Highlights importance of bias testing requirements
